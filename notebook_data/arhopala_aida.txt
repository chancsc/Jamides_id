Arhopala aida is a member of the alitaeus group. Its identification on the wing underside is characterized by a specific wing shape and the presence of a distinct glaze.

Underside Identification Keys

Forewing Shape: The forewing is distinctive within its group for having a straight termen and a slightly produced apex.

Subspecies and Glaze: The ground color and glaze vary by region:
Subspecies aida (Langkawi): Features a paler brown ground color with a strong, slatey grey glaze.

Subspecies ophir (Mount Ophir): Features a pale purple glaze.

General Mainland Population: The slatey glaze is typically only weakly developed.

Forewing Spotting: It possesses a central spot in space 11 but lacks a spot at the extreme base of space 10 (above the central cell spot).

Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6 Position

Alignment: As a member of the alitaeus group, the centers of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement: The inner edge of spot 6 is positioned in line with, or even further inside (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Overlapping: The spot typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: The black border expands from approximately 1.25 mm at the tornus to 2.0 mm at the apex.
