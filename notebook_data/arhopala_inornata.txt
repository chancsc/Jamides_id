Arhopala inornata (Inornate Oakblue) is the sole member of the inornata group. It is characterized by its rounded wing shape and specific spot alignments on the underside.

Underside Identification Keys

Tail Status: It is a tailless species.

Hindwing Shape: The hindwing is very rounded, particularly at the tornus.

Tornal Area: It lacks green tornal scales on the hindwing.

Band Dislocation: The postdiscal band on the hindwing is completely dislocated at vein 2.

Marking Definition: The markings follow the typical Arhopala pattern but are specifically aligned as detailed below.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The centers of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement: The spot in space 6 is positioned immediately below the spot in space 7. 

Inner Edge: Its inner edge is positioned in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Overlapping: The spot typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Bright violet blue with the black border reduced to a thread.

Female: Bluer than the male with exceptionally narrow borders; the hindwing border is only about 0.5 mm wide between veins 2 and 6.
