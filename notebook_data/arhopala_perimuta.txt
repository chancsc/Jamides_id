Arhopala perimuta regina is a member of the amphimuta group and the perimuta subgroup. Its identification on the underside is defined by the following keys:

Underside Identification Keys

Central Yellowish Area: The hindwing underside is characterized by a distinctive central yellowish area.

Tornal Area: It lacks green tornal scales, which helps distinguish it from the similar Arhopala metamuta.

Band Dislocation: The postdiscal band on the hindwing is completely dislocated at vein 2.

Forewing Markings: The postdiscal spots in spaces 2 and 3 on the forewing are arranged in line with each other (though they may be oblique).

Genitalia: The male possesses a bilobate valva.

Size: It is a relatively small species with a forewing length of approximately 17.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Echelon Alignment: As a member of the amphimuta group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 5, 6, and 7 are arranged in an echelon (more or less in a straight line) and are overlapping.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned directly below the spot in space 7.

Proximity: The spot in space 6 is positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.
