Arhopala barami penanga is a large, tailed species belonging to the agelastus group and agelastus subgroup. Its underside is characterized by a specific arrangement of submarginal and postdiscal markings.

Underside Identification Keys

Forewing Markings: 
The postdiscal band is continuous.

It lacks a costal spot in space 10.

A small spot is often present at the base of space 3.

Marking Uniformity: All inner submarginal spots on the underside are of the same size and colour.

Tail: Unlike most other members of its subgroup, it is a tailed species.

Size: Relatively large for the group, with a forewing length of 22.0–23.0 mm.

Position and Shape of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement: The inner edge of the spot in space 6 is positioned in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Overlap: The spot in space 6 typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Shape: The spot is notably outwardly convex.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Blue with exceptionally broad black forewing borders, measuring 2.5 mm at the tornus and increasing to 8.0 mm at the costa.

Female: Paler blue with similarly broad black borders.
