Arhopala achelous belongs to the anthelus group and the achelous subgroup. Its identification on the underside is characterized by its hair brown ground color and specific spot alignments.

Underside Identification Keys

Forewing Postdiscal Band: The postdiscal band is only partially dislocated at vein 4.

Forewing Spots: Unlike the closely related Arhopala anthelus, it lacks spots in space 11 of the forewing.

Hindwing Spots: There is only a single spot at the base of space 8 (whereas A. anthelus has two).

Ground Colour: The underside is typically hair brown with a purple wash. However, the Kedawi subspecies A. achelous malu lacks most of this purple tinge.

Size: It is smaller than A. anthelus, with a forewing length of approximately 22.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon, with their centers more or less in a straight line.

Placement: The spot in space 6 is positioned nearer to the spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.
