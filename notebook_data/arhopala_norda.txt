Arhopala norda is a member of the amphimuta group and the amphimuta subgroup. It is a relatively large, tailless species that can be easily confused with Arhopala major.

Underside Identification Keys

Hindwing Cilia: A key diagnostic feature is the presence of a small white patch on the hindwing cilia located just above the tornal lobe.

Band Dislocation: The postdiscal band on the hindwing is partially dislocated at vein 2.

Spot Shape: Compared to Arhopala major, the postdiscal spots are less rounded.

Structural Features: It is a tailless species with a very weak tooth at vein 2 and a small white spot in space 1a of the hindwing cilia.

Ground Color: The underside is uniform hair brown.

Subspecies: A. norda norda is found in peninsular Malaysia, while A. norda ronda occurs in the Langkawi Islands.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the amphimuta group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon (more or less in a straight line) and are overlapping.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Violet blue with a black border that expands from 2.0 mm at the forewing tornus to 3.0 mm at the apex. The Langkawi subspecies ronda is notably bluer than populations found elsewhere in Sundaland.
