Jamides lugine is a Bornean endemic species belonging to the celeno group and cyta subgroup. It is primarily found in lowland primeval forest habitats.

Underside Identification Keys

Marking Style: As a member of the celeno group, it features straight white striae.

Pattern Characteristics: The underside pattern and colour approach that of Jamides bochus. 

Submarginal Area: The spaces between the inner and outer submarginal striae are barely darker than the ground colour, which distinguishes it from other members of its subgroup like Jamides cyta.

Band Alignment: On the forewing, the postdiscal band in space 3 is positioned more or less in line with the band in spaces 4 and 5.

Upperside Identification Keys

Male: Dull purple upperside. The black border on the forewing is narrow. A key structural feature is that the male lacks androconia (scent scales).

Female: Paler purple upperside compared to the male.

Size and Tail Information

Wing Length: The forewing length typically ranges between 14.0 mm and 17.0 mm.

Tail: Like other members of its genus, it possesses a filamentous tail at the end of vein 2 on the hindwing.
