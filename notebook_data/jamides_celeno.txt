Jamides celeno aelianus (The Common Caerulean) is the most widely distributed and abundant member of its genus in the Malay Peninsula. It is found in a variety of habitats, including gardens, secondary growth, and open forest at elevations below 1,200 m.

Underside Identification Keys

Group Characteristics: As a member of the celeno group, it features straight white striae (lines) rather than the lunulate buff striae seen in the bochus group.

Subgroup Characteristics: Within the celeno subgroup, the postdiscal band on the forewing is continuous from vein 3 to vein 7.

Hindwing Pattern: It is distinguished by having only a single subbasal pair of continuous, straight white lines on the hindwing.

Striae Color: The striae are white to whitish on a greyish ground colour.

Upperside Identification Keys

Male: Whitish blue upperside. The black border on the forewing is comparatively broad, increasing to at least 1.0 mm at the apex.

Female: Characteristically dingy whitish blue, which is less clear and bright than related species like J. pura or J. zebra.

Size and Structural Features

Size: A small to moderate-sized butterfly.

Tail: Possesses a filamentous tail at the end of vein 2 on the hindwing.
