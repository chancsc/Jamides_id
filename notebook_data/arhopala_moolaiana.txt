Arhopala moolaiana (the Yellow-disc Tailless Oakblue) belongs to the amphimuta group and the amphimuta subgroup. It is a common species that closely resembles Arhopala amphimuta and Arhopala major on the underside.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: The underside is a uniform hair brown.

Structural Features: It is a tailless species, although the hindwing has a very weak tooth at vein 2 and a small white spot in space 1a of the hindwing cilia.

Hindwing Band Dislocation: The postdiscal band on the hindwing is partially dislocated at vein 2.

Forewing Markings: The postdiscal band is slightly dislocated at vein 4.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the amphimuta group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon (more or less in a straight line) and are overlapping.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is specifically positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Shining blue to violet blue with the black border reduced to a thread.

Female: Blue with a very narrow hindwing border, usually only about 1.0 mm wide between veins 2 and 6. The wings are described as quadrate.
