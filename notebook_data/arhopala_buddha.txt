Arhopala buddha cooperi is a rare member of the rama group. Its identification on the underside is characterized by its short tail and specific spot arrangements.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Color: The underside is a uniform hair brown.

Tail: It possesses a very short hindwing tail, measuring approximately 1.0 mm in length.

Tornal Area: The hindwing features distinct green tornal scales.

Forewing Markings: A costal spot in space 10 is almost always present on the forewing underside.

Hindwing Alignment: As a member of the rama group, the postdiscal spot in space 6 has a characteristic sinuous shape.

Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6 Position

Alignment: The centers of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement: The inner edge of the spot in space 6 is positioned in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

End-cell Bar: The spot in space 6 typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Shape: The outer edge of the spot is notably outwardly sinuous.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Shining violet blue with a black border approximately 1.0 mm wide.

Female: Brighter blue with a very narrow black border on the hindwing; it may be confused with the female of Arhopala aurea, but A. buddha has a narrower hindwing border and a much less robust tail.
