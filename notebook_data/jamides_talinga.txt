Jamides talinga talinga is the smallest Malayan species within the elpis subgroup of the celeno group. It is found in forested areas of the mainland and is also recorded from Kinabalu Park in Sabah.

Underside Identification Keys

Subgroup Characteristic: As a member of the elpis subgroup, the postdiscal band on the forewing underside is completely dislocated at vein 3.

Band Alignment: The postdiscal band is slightly dislocated at vein 6, with the portion in space 6 shifted basad (inward).

Costal Markings: The underside of the forewing usually features a pair of distinct costal white dashes located above the end-cell bar.

Hindwing Pattern: The subbasal striae (lines) on the hindwing are characteristically dislocated rather than continuous.

Upperside Identification Keys

Male: The upperside is a shining blue. It is distinguished by a comparatively broad black border on the forewing, which measures 1.0 mm at the tornus and increases to at least 2.0 mm at the apex. The male lacks androconia (scent scales).

Female: The upperside is a pale, silky blue.

Size and Tail Information

Wing Length: It is a small species with a forewing length (fwl) of less than 15.0 mm.

Tail: Like other members of the genus Jamides, it possesses a filamentous tail at the end of vein 2 on the hindwing.
