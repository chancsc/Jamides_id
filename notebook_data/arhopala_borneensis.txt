Arhopala borneensis belongs to the aurea group. Its underside pattern is noted to exactly match that of Arhopala aurea, characterized by specific spot widths and a long hindwing cell.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: The underside is a uniform hair brown.

Hindwing Cell: The hindwing cell is exceptionally long, measuring more than half the length of the wing.

Forewing Markings: The postdiscal spots in spaces 2 and 3 are wider than those located above vein 4, with the spot in space 3 being particularly wide.

Hindwing Band Dislocation: The postdiscal band on the hindwing is only partially dislocated at vein 2.

Structural Features: It possesses a short and stout tail at vein 2, measuring just under 2.0 mm.

Space 1b: The dark patch typically found under the cell in space 1b is not solid.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement: The inner edge of the spot in space 6 is positioned in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Overlap: The spot in space 6 typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Brassy green with a strong blue suffusion at the wing bases. The black forewing border is a thread throughout. On the hindwing, the green area usually reaches the termen in spaces 5 and/or 6.

Female: Blue with a black hindwing border that reaches at least halfway to the end of the cell.
