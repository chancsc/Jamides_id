Jamides alecto ageladas (The Metallic Caerulean) is a member of the celeno group and the elpis subgroup. It is noted as being the largest Malayan representative of its subgroup.

Underside Identification Keys

Subgroup Characteristic: As a member of the elpis subgroup, the postdiscal band on the forewing underside is completely dislocated at vein 3.

Marking Style: The white markings (striae) on the underside are slightly broader and more diffuse than in other related species.

Band Alignment: The postdiscal band on the forewing is slightly dislocated at vein 6, with the portion in space 6 shifted basad (inward).

Upperside Identification Keys

Male: The upperside is blue with a diffuse black border on the forewing that expands to approximately 1.0 mm at the apex. A key diagnostic feature is the presence of a series of black marginal spots on the hindwing. The male lacks androconia (scent scales).

Female: The upperside is blue, and the black border on the forewing is distinctive for extending narrowly along the costa to the wing base.

Size and Structural Features

Size: It is a moderate-sized species, characterized as the largest in the elpis subgroup. Its forewing length is typically greater than 15.0 mm.

Tail: It possesses a filamentous tail at the end of vein 2 on the hindwing.
