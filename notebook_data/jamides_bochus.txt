Jamides bochus nabonassar (The Dark Caerulean) is the sole representative of the bochus group in the Malay Peninsula. It is distinguished from other members of the genus by its more active, fast-flying habits and distinctive metallic coloration.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: The underside ground colour is described as greyish brown or ochreous brown.

Marking Style: Features pale buff, lunulate (comma-like) striae. These markings are characteristically narrow, dull, and rather indistinct. 

Forewing Band Alignment: The postdiscal band in space 3 is positioned more or less in line with the band in spaces 4 and 5.

Group Distinction: The bochus group is identified by these lunulate, pale buff striae, whereas the celeno group features straight white striae.

Upperside Identification Keys

Male: Brilliant dark metallic morpho blue. The black bordering on the forewing is very broad and enters the upper portion of the cell. The male lacks androconia (scent scales).

Female: Non-metallic blue. Like the male, the broad black bordering on the forewing enters the upper portion of the cell.

Size and Structural Features

Size: As a member of the genus Jamides, it is a small to moderate-sized butterfly.

Tail: Possesses a filamentous tail at the end of vein 2 on the hindwing.
