Arhopala muta (Raffles's Oakblue) is a member of the amphimuta group and the muta subgroup. It is characterized by specific spot alignments and a lack of a tail.

Underside Identification Keys

Hindwing Spot 6 Position:
As a member of the amphimuta group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 5, 6, and 7 are overlapping and arranged more or less in a line (in echelon).

The postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned nearer to the spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Spot Shape and Alignment: The markings on the underside are more quadrate and banded compared to related subgroups.

Forewing Postdiscal Band: The postdiscal spot in space 2 is distinctly stepped out towards the termen, placing it in echelon with the spot in space 3.

Hindwing Band Dislocation: The postdiscal band is dislocated such that the inner edge of the spot in space 2 is in line with, or only slightly outside, the outer edge of the spot in space 1b.

Tail Status: It is a tailless species.

Cilia: The hindwing cilia lack a white spot in space 1a.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: The upperside forewing is a rather dark, bright blue with a black border that increases from 1.0–1.5 mm at the tornus to 1.5–2.0 mm at the apex. The hindwing is typically a slightly paler and brighter blue.

Female: Shining blue with only a slight purple tint and broad black borders.
