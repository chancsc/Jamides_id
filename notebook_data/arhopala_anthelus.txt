Arhopala anthelus (the Anthelus Oakblue) is a member of the anthelus group and the anthelus subgroup. It is distinguished by its unique extra spotting and large size.

Underside Identification Keys

Forewing Postdiscal Band: The postdiscal band is only partially dislocated at vein 4.

Forewing Spots: It possesses three spots in space 11 of the forewing.

Hindwing Spots: There are two spots at the base of space 8 (distinguishing it from Arhopala achelous, which has only one).

Marking Coloration: The spots on the forewing and in the upper half of the hindwing are a distinct deep purple brown.

Size: It is a large species with a forewing length of approximately 28.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the anthelus group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon (more or less in a straight line).

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned nearer to the spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.
