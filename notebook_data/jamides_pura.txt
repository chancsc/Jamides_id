Jamides pura pura (The White Caerulean) is a forest-dwelling member of the celeno group and celeno subgroup. It is found in lowland forest habitats ranging from the Langkawi Islands to Singapore.

Underside Identification Keys

Marking Style: As a member of the celeno group, it features straight white striae (lines) rather than lunulate ones.

Forewing Band: The postdiscal band on the forewing is continuous from vein 3 to vein 7.

Hindwing Pattern: It is characterized by having only a single subbasal pair of straight white lines on the hindwing.

Visibility: In the male, the underside markings are often visible from the upperside by transparency.

Upperside Identification Keys

Male: Clear, unicolorous blue. The black border on the forewing is reduced to a thread-like line.

Female: Pale, clear blue. It is brighter and clearer in appearance compared to the dingy whitish blue of Jamides celeno.

Size and Structural Features

Size: A small to moderate-sized butterfly.

Tail: Possesses a filamentous tail at the end of vein 2 on the hindwing.
