Jamides aratus adana is a member of the celeno group and celeno subgroup. In the Malay Peninsula region, it is quite common on Pulau Tioman and other East Coast islands but has never been recorded on the mainland.

Underside Identification Keys

Marking Style: As a member of the celeno group, it features straight white striae (lines) rather than lunulate buff ones.

Forewing Band Alignment: The postdiscal band in space 3 is positioned more or less in line with the band in spaces 4 and 5. The band is continuous from vein 3 to vein 7.

Hindwing Pattern: A key diagnostic feature is the presence of prominent black, wedge-shaped spots in the spaces between the inner and outer submarginal striae. This pattern is shared with Jamides philatus, but the two are easily distinguished by their upperside colouration.

Upperside Identification Keys

Colour: Both sexes are almost white upperside, which distinguishes them from the blue-coloured Jamides philatus.

Male: The upperside of the forewing features a diffuse black border nearly 1.0 mm wide. The hindwing possesses a double series of dark submarginal spots.

Female: Similar to the male in its nearly white ground colour.

Size and Structural Features

Size: It is a small to moderate-sized butterfly.

Tail: It possesses a filamentous tail at the end of vein 2 on the hindwing.
