Jamides abdul abdul is a member of the celeno group and the elpis subgroup. It is a forest butterfly found in the Malay Peninsula and has been recorded in Singapore.

Underside Identification Keys

Subgroup Characteristic: As a member of the elpis subgroup, the postdiscal band on the forewing underside is completely dislocated at vein 3.

Band Alignment: The postdiscal band on the forewing is slightly curved but not dislocated at vein 6. This distinguishes it from many other members of the subgroup where the portion in space 6 is shifted basad (inward).

Marking Definition: All white markings (striae) are clearly defined on the underside.

Upperside Identification Keys

Male: Distinctive for having a coppery green upperside with very broad, diffuse black borders. The male lacks androconia (scent scales).

Female: The upperside is blue, and the black border on the forewing is notable for extending broadly along the costa to the wing base.

Size and Structural Features

Size: It is a small to moderate-sized butterfly.

Tail: Like other members of the genus Jamides, it possesses a filamentous tail at the end of vein 2 on the hindwing.
