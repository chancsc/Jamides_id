Jamides cunilda nisanca is a member of the celeno group and the elpis subgroup. It is found in forested areas across the Malay Peninsula and on various islands, including Pulau Tioman, Pulau Aur, and Pulau Pemanggil.

Underside Identification Keys

Subgroup Characteristic: As a member of the elpis subgroup, the postdiscal band on the forewing underside is completely dislocated at vein 3.

Band Alignment: The postdiscal band on the forewing is slightly dislocated at vein 6, with the portion in space 6 shifted basad (inward).

Costal Markings: The underside of the forewing usually features a pair of distinct costal white dashes located above the end-cell bar.

Tornal Area: The orange tornal area on the hindwing is comparatively large, often obliterating the outer postdiscal stria in space 2 and sometimes in space 3.

Marking Definition: All white markings (striae) are narrow and clearly defined.

Upperside Identification Keys

Male: The upperside is purple blue. The black border on the forewing is usually a thread-like line but may sometimes expand slightly and diffusely at the apex. The male lacks androconia (scent scales).

Female: The upperside is a much paler, slightly chalky blue. The black border on the forewing is diffuse and characteristically expands abruptly just above vein 4. The hindwing features a series of rather small and diffuse submarginal spots on a bluish ground.

Size and Structural Features

Size: It is a small to moderate-sized butterfly.

Tail: It possesses a filamentous tail at the end of vein 2 on the hindwing.
