Jamides yehi is an exceptionally rare member of the celeno group and celeno subgroup. It was described based on a single damaged male specimen collected from Tapah Hills, Perak, which was missing its abdomen, antennae, and legs.

Underside Identification Keys

Marking Style: The underside is described as atypical for the genus Jamides. 

Ground Colour: The ground colour is white.

Markings: It features blackish brown markings rather than the typical white striae.

Tornal Spot: Despite the atypical pattern, it retains the characteristic black tornal spot crowned with orange on the hindwing.

Band Alignment: The postdiscal band in space 3 is positioned more or less in line with the band in spaces 4 and 5. The band is continuous from vein 3 to vein 7.

Upperside Identification Keys

Androconia: Unlike most other members of the celeno subgroup, the male of Jamides yehi lacks battledore androconia (scent scales).

Size and Structural Features

Wing Length: The forewing length is 19.0 mm.

Tail: It is a tailed species, possessing a filamentous tail at the end of vein 2 on the hindwing.
