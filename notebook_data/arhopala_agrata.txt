Arhopala agrata agrata (de Nicéville's Dull Oakblue) belongs to the agrata group and agrata subgroup. Its identification on the underside is based on specific spot alignments and the absence of certain markings.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: The underside is uniform hair brown.

Marking Definition: All markings are notably faint.

Forewing Markings: 
The postdiscal spot in space 9 is either absent or very obscure.

It never possesses a costal spot in space 10.

Hindwing Markings: The postdiscal band is completely dislocated at vein 2.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Width: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is considerably wider than the spot in space 7.

Placement: The inner edge of the spot in space 6 is positioned in line with, or even further inside, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Overlapping: The spot typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Dark violet blue with a black border reduced to a thread.

Female: Purplish blue. It can be distinguished from similar species because the blue colour on the hindwing reaches the termen between the vein endings.
