Jamides limes is a Bornean species belonging to the celeno group and the elpis subgroup. It is notably larger than many of its close relatives.

Underside Identification Keys

Subgroup Characteristic: As a member of the elpis subgroup, the postdiscal band on the forewing underside is completely dislocated at vein 3.

Striae Arrangement: The second white stria from the base in space 7 on the hindwing is positioned about midway between the second and third striae from the base in the cell.

Marking Style: The white striae (lines) are characteristically broad and rather diffuse.

Costal Markings: The underside of the forewing usually features costal white dashes located above the end-cell bar.

Tornal Area: It possesses a large orange tornal area on the hindwing.

Upperside Identification Keys

Male: The upperside is a shining blue with a thin black border. A key structural feature is that the male lacks androconia (scent scales).

Female: The upperside is also blue with darker borders.

Size and Tail Information

Wing Length: It is a large species within its group, with a forewing length (fwl) of at least 18.0 mm.

Tail: Like other members of the genus, it possesses a filamentous tail at the end of vein 2 on the hindwing.
