Arhopala athada athada is a common and widespread member of the cleander group and cleander subgroup. It is a relatively large species that can be identified by the following underside features:

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour: The underside is a uniform hair brown.

Forewing Markings: 
The postdiscal spot in space 9 is usually narrower than the spot in space 6 and is shifted basad (inward), placing it out of line with the spots in spaces 4 to 6.

A costal spot in space 10 is typically present and is placed over the outer edge of the end-cell bar.

Hindwing Markings: 
The postdiscal band is completely dislocated at vein 2.

It features a large and conspicuous tornal lobe.

Size: Forewing length measures approximately 22.0–23.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The postdiscal spots in spaces 6 and 7 are usually more or less upright, with their outer edges in line or nearly so.

Shape: Spot 6 is more or less quadrate.

Placement: The spot usually just about fills the gap between the spot in space 5 and the end-cell bar.

Overlapping: It often overlaps the end-cell bar and occasionally the spot in space 5. 

Diagnostic Gap: To distinguish it from Arhopala silhetensis and Arhopala cleander, note that the gap between the spot in space 5 and the end-cell bar is not usually wider than the width of either of those spots.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Purple blue with a black border that is a little wider than that of Arhopala zambra.

Female: Typical of the genus with broad black borders.
