Arhopala stinga is a member of the aurea group. Its underside identification is highly specific, as it is noted to exactly resemble the underside of Arhopala trogon.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Colour and Wash: The underside is brown with a prominent and distinctive purple wash.

Hindwing Cell: The hindwing cell is exceptionally long, measuring considerably more than half the length of the wing.

Forewing Markings: 
The postdiscal spots in spaces 2 and 3 are wider than those located above vein 4.

The dark patch typically found under the cell in space 1b is not solid.

Hindwing Band Dislocation: The postdiscal band on the hindwing is only partially dislocated at vein 2.

Tail: It possesses a short and stout tail at vein 2, measuring just under 2.0 mm.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

The arrangement of the postdiscal spots on the hindwing is a primary diagnostic feature:
*   Alignment: The centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.
*   Placement: The inner edge of the spot in space 6 is positioned in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.
*   End-cell Bar: The spot in space 6 typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Brilliant green, described as being less brassy than A. trogon and lacking the basal blue suffusion. The black border on the forewing is relatively wide, measuring 2.5–3.0 mm at the tornus and decreasing to a thread at vein 6. On the hindwing, the green area reaches the end of the cell.

Female: Resembles the female of Arhopala aurea, typically being a shade of blue with broad black borders.
