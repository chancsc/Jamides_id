Jamides ferrari evansi (The Ferrari’s Caerulean) is a very rare member of the celeno group and the elpis subgroup. It is found in forested areas of the Malay Peninsula.

Underside Identification Keys

Subgroup Characteristic: As a member of the elpis subgroup, the postdiscal band on the forewing underside is completely dislocated at vein 3.

Marking Style: The markings are narrower and neater than those of its larger relative, Jamides alecto.

Costal Markings: Unlike some members of the subgroup, the underside of the forewing typically lacks costal white dashes above the end-cell bar.

Hindwing Pattern: The orange tornal area on the hindwing is relatively small and does not reach the postdiscal stria in space 2.

Upperside Identification Keys

Male: The upperside is a paler blue compared to related species. The black border on the forewing expands to approximately 1.0 mm at the apex. The hindwing possesses distinct submarginal markings. The male possesses battledore androconia (scent scales).

Female: Features a regular black border on the forewing. The hindwing has well-defined submarginal spots on a whitish ground, with the spot in space 2 being characteristically linear.

Size and Structural Features

Size: It is a moderate-sized butterfly with a forewing length typically greater than 15.0 mm.

Tail: Like other species in the genus, it possesses a filamentous tail at the end of vein 2 on the hindwing.
