Arhopala amphimuta amphimuta is the nominate member of the amphimuta group and the amphimuta subgroup. It is a relatively large, tailless species that is common and widespread in the Malay Peninsula.

Underside Identification Keys

Hindwing Spot 6 Position:
As a member of the amphimuta group, the postdiscal spots in spaces 5, 6, and 7 are arranged in an echelon (more or less in a straight line) and are overlapping.

The postdiscal spot in space 6 is specifically positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Forewing Postdiscal Band: 
The postdiscal band is slightly dislocated at vein 4.

The spot in space 3 is usually an oblique oval.

Structural Features: 
It is a tailless species.

The hindwing has a very weak tooth at vein 2 and a small white spot in space 1a of the hindwing cilia.

Male Genitalia: The male possesses a bilobate valva, which helps distinguish it from unilobate species within the same group.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Rich violet purple with narrow, regular black borders measuring just over 0.5 mm wide.

Female: A lighter purple with the typical broad black borders found in the genus.
