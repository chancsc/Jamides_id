Jamides cyta minna (The Pale Caerulean) is a relatively large member of its genus found in the Malay Peninsula and is the sole representative of its specific subgroup in the region.

Underside Identification Keys

Group and Subgroup: It belongs to the celeno group (characterized by straight white striae) and is the only local member of the cyta subgroup.

Diagnostic Feature: It is uniquely distinguished by having the spaces between the inner and outer submarginal striae on the hindwing filled with dark brown (strongly darkened).

Band Alignment: On the forewing, the postdiscal band in space 3 is positioned more or less in line with the spots in spaces 4 and 5.

Upperside Identification Keys

Male: Blue upperside. A key structural feature is the presence of long paddle-shaped androconia (scent scales), which differ significantly from the battledore androconia found in other subgroups.

Female: Blue upperside.

Size and Structural Features

Size: It is described as one of the largest species within the genus Jamides.

Tail: Like other members of the genus, it possesses a filamentous tail at the end of vein 2 on the hindwing.
