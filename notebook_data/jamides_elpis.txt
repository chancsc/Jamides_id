Jamides elpis pseudelpis (The Glistening Caerulean) is a member of the celeno group and the elpis subgroup. It is found in forested areas and is often seen flying around shrubs and bushes.

Underside Identification Keys

Subgroup Characteristic: As a member of the elpis subgroup, the postdiscal band on the forewing underside is completely dislocated at vein 3.

Striae Arrangement: The second white stria from the base in space 7 on the hindwing is positioned about midway between the second and third striae from the base in the cell.

Marking Definition: The white striae are narrow and clearly defined.

Tornal Area: The orange tornal area on the hindwing is not unusually large and does not reach the postdiscal stria in space 2.

Upperside Identification Keys

Male: Pale shining sky-blue. The black border on the forewing is reduced to a thread. A key feature is that the underside markings are visible from the upperside by transparency.

Female: Pale blue upperside. It possesses a prominent black tornal spot on the hindwing which is sometimes faintly orange-crowned. The black border on the forewing ends at the mid-costa.

Size and Tail Information

Wing Length: It is a relatively small species with a forewing length (fwl) of less than 18.0 mm.

Tail: It possesses a filamentous tail at the end of vein 2 on the hindwing.
