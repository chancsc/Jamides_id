Jamides philatus subditus is an uncommon member of the celeno group and celeno subgroup. It is found in forest habitats in the Malay Peninsula and Singapore.

Underside Identification Keys

Marking Style: As a member of the celeno group, it features straight white striae.

Submarginal Markings: A key diagnostic feature is the presence of prominent black, wedge-shaped spots in the spaces between the inner and outer submarginal striae on the hindwing.

Band Alignment: The postdiscal band in space 3 on the forewing is positioned more or less in line with the band in spaces 4 and 5.

Striae Color: The striae are white to whitish on a greyish ground colour.

Upperside Identification Keys

Male: Sky blue. The black border on the forewing is reduced to a thread, and the hindwing lacks submarginal spots.

Female: Pale blue.

Size and Structural Features

Size: A small to moderate-sized butterfly.

Tail: It possesses a filamentous tail at the end of vein 2 on the hindwing.
