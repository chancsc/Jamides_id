Arhopala silhetensis adorea (the Sylhet Oakblue) is a large member of the cleander group and the cleander subgroup. It is characterized by its prominent tornal lobe and specific spot alignments on the underside.

Underside Identification Keys

Forewing Markings:
The postdiscal band is partially dislocated at vein 4.

The postdiscal spot in space 9 is usually narrower than the spot in space 6 and is shifted basad (inward), out of line with the spots in spaces 4 to 6.

A costal spot in space 10 is usually present and is typically placed over the outer edge of the end-cell bar.

Hindwing Markings:
The postdiscal band is completely dislocated at vein 2.

The gap between the postdiscal spot in space 5 and the end-cell bar is wider than either of these markings.

Structural Features: The hindwing possesses a conspicuously projecting tornal lobe.

Size: It is a relatively large species with a forewing length of 23.0–26.0 mm.

Position and Shape of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement: The spot in space 6 is placed well inside the inner edge of the spot in space 5. 

Overlap: The inner edge of the spot in space 6 does not overlap the end-cell bar.

Shape: The spot is wider at the top than at the bottom, and its outer edge is convex or sinuous.

Relation to Spot 7: The arrangement is oblique, with the inner edge of the spot in space 7 positioned well inside the outer edge of the spot in space 6.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Violet blue with a black border that is usually not more than 1.0 mm wide.

Female: Violet blue with the usual broad black borders.
