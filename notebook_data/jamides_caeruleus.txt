Jamides caeruleus caeruleus (The Metallic Caerulean) is a common and widely distributed member of the celeno group and elpis subgroup.

Underside Identification Keys

Subgroup Characteristic: As part of the elpis subgroup, the postdiscal band on the forewing underside is completely dislocated at vein 3.

Band Alignment: The postdiscal band on the forewing is slightly dislocated at vein 6, with the portion in space 6 shifted basad (inward).

Hindwing Pattern: A key diagnostic feature is the arrangement of the subbasal striae. The second white stria from the base in space 7 is in line with the third stria from the base in the cell (or at least closer to it than to the second cell stria).

Costal Markings: Unlike some similar species, the underside of the forewing usually lacks costal white dashes above the end-cell bar.

Upperside Identification Keys

Male: Brilliant metallic caerulean blue. The black border on the forewing increases to about 1.0 mm at the apex. The male lacks battledore androconia (scent scales).

Female: The upperside blue is deeper and more shining than in other species of the elpis subgroup.

Size and Tail Information

Wing Length: It is a moderate-sized species with a forewing length typically greater than 15.0 mm.

Tail: It possesses a filamentous tail at the end of vein 2 on the hindwing.
