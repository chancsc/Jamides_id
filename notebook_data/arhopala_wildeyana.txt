Arhopala wildeyana wildeyana is a member of the agelastus group and the agelastus subgroup. It is a relatively small, tailless species characterized by the following underside identification keys:

Underside Identification Keys

Forewing Markings:
The postdiscal band on the forewing is continuous.

It lacks a costal spot in space 10.

A small spot is often present at the base of space 3.

Hindwing Markings:
The postdiscal band is only partially dislocated at vein 2.

The hindwing possesses tornal green scales.

The cilia are dentate at vein 2.

A distinctive feature is that the postdiscal spot in space 3 is usually shifted well in (basad), placing it out of line with the spots in spaces 2 and 4.

Size: It is a small species with a forewing length of approximately 16.0 mm.

Position and Shape of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: The centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement: The inner edge of the spot in space 6 is positioned in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Overlap: The spot in space 6 typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Shape: The spot is outwardly convex.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Violet blue with a black border that is 2.0 mm at the tornus and increases to 5.0 mm at the costa.

Female: Brighter and purer blue than the male, possessing the typical broad black borders.
