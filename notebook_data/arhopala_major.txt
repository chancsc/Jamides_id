Arhopala major major (the Major Oakblue) is a member of the amphimuta group and the amphimuta subgroup. It is a relatively large, tailless species characterized by the following underside features:

Underside Identification Keys

Hindwing Cilia: The cilia feature a small white spot in space 1a. Additionally, there is a small white patch on the hindwing cilia located just above the tornal lobe.

Hindwing Tooth: The hindwing has a very weak tooth at vein 2 (inconspicuous in worn specimens).

Hindwing Band Dislocation: The postdiscal band is partially dislocated at vein 2.

Marking Shape: The postdiscal spots are generally more rounded compared to closely related species like Arhopala norda.

Male Genitalia: The male possesses a bilobate valva, which is a key structural character used to distinguish it from unilobate species in the same subgroup.

Ground Colour: The underside is a uniform hair brown.

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the amphimuta group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are arranged in an echelon (more or less in a straight line) and are overlapping.

Placement: The postdiscal spot in space 6 is positioned nearer to the postdiscal spot in space 5 than to the end-cell bar.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Violet blue with a black border that expands from 2.0 mm at the forewing tornus to 3.0 mm at the apex. It is generally bluer than Arhopala amphimuta.

Female: Bluer than the male with the typical broad black borders.
