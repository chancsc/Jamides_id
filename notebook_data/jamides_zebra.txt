Jamides zebra lakatti (The Zebra Caerulean) is a forest-dwelling butterfly belonging to the celeno group and celeno subgroup. It is found in primary forest habitats and is brighter and clearer in appearance than the common Jamides celeno.

Underside Identification Keys

Marking Style: As a member of the celeno group, it features straight white striae (lines) instead of the lunulate ones seen in the bochus group.

Forewing Band: The postdiscal band on the forewing underside is continuous from vein 3 to vein 7.

Hindwing Pattern: It possesses only a single subbasal pair of continuous, straight white lines on the hindwing.

Transparency: Unlike its close relative Jamides pura, the underside markings of Jamides zebra are barely visible from the upperside by transparency.

Upperside Identification Keys

Male: Clearer blue than Jamides celeno. A key diagnostic feature is that the blue color on the forewing deepens towards the apex, and the black border expands slightly and diffusely in that region.

Female: Brighter and clearer blue than the dingy whitish blue of Jamides celeno.

Size and Structural Features

Size: It is a small to moderate-sized butterfly.

Tail: It possesses a filamentous tail at the end of vein 2 on the hindwing.
