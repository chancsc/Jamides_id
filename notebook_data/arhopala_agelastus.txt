Arhopala agelastus agelastus belongs to the agelastus group and the agelastus subgroup. It is a relatively rare species, characterized by its specific postdiscal alignments and lack of certain costal markings.

Underside Identification Keys

Ground Color: The underside is typically a uniform hair brown.

Forewing Markings:
The postdiscal band is continuous.

It lacks a costal spot in space 10.

A small spot is usually present at the base of space 3.

Hindwing Markings:
The postdiscal band is only partially dislocated at vein 2.

The hindwing possesses tornal green scales.

The cilia are dentate at vein 2.

Crucial Alignment: The postdiscal spots in spaces 2, 3, 4, and 5 form an almost even curve. This helps distinguish it from related species where the spot in space 3 is often shifted inward (basad).

Position of Hindwing Postdiscal Spot 6

Alignment: As a member of the agelastus group, the centres of the postdiscal spots in spaces 7, 6, and 5 are widely out of line.

Placement: The inner edge of the spot in space 6 is positioned in line with, or even further inward (basad) than, the inner edge of the spot in space 7.

Overlap: The spot in space 6 typically touches or overlaps the end-cell bar.

Shape: The spot is notably outwardly convex.

Upperside Characteristics

Male: Violet blue with a black border that is 1.0 mm at the tornus and increases to 4.0 mm at the apex.

Female: Bluer than the male with the typical broad black borders.
